Stroke Information and Prevention

What is a Stroke?

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of your brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain cells begin to die within minutes. There are two main types of stroke:

  • Ischemic Stroke: Caused by a blockage in an artery (about 87% of strokes).
  • Hemorrhagic Stroke: Caused by a blood vessel bursting in the brain.

Symptoms of a stroke include sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg (especially on one side of the body), sudden confusion, trouble speaking, or difficulty understanding speech, sudden trouble seeing, dizziness, or a severe headache.

Stroke Prevention Tips

  • Control Blood Pressure: High blood pressure is a leading cause of stroke. Monitor your blood pressure regularly and manage it through diet, exercise, and medication if necessary.
  • Maintain a Healthy Diet: Eat a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Reduce salt, saturated fats, and processed foods.
  • Exercise Regularly: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week, such as brisk walking or cycling.
  • Quit Smoking: Smoking increases the risk of stroke. Seek support to quit if needed.
  • Limit Alcohol: Excessive alcohol can raise blood pressure. Limit to one drink per day for women and two for men.
  • Manage Diabetes: Keep blood sugar levels under control through diet, exercise, and medication.
  • Know the Signs: Learn the FAST method to recognize stroke symptoms: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services.
